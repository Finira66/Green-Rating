What is not called Green Energy?
In order to be deemed green energy, a resource cannot produce pollution, such as is found with fossil fuels. This means that not all sources used by the renewable energy industry are green. For example, power generation that burns organic material from sustainable forests may be renewable, but it is not necessarily green, due to the CO2 produced by the burning process itself.
Green sources also try to avoid mining or drilling operations that can be damaging to eco-systems.
Green energyâ€™s sources are historically considered inexhaustible. The essence of this method is to obtain energy from the processes constantly occurring in nature with its subsequent application in the technical sphere.
Why do we all need Green Energy?
We already know the answer.
The energy problem today is one of the most pressing for all mankind. Traditional sources such as oil, gas and other fossil fuels are gradually losing their relevance as they cause enormous damage to the environment. That is why all kinds of solar panels, wind and hydroelectric power plants, as well as bioreactors are becoming so popular today.
The main problem with conventional resources at the moment is their limited quantity. And since the "appetite" of mankind in this respect is growing exponentially, in the near future, according to many scientists, people may already face a crisis. So, this is the thing which is about every person in the world.
Green energy also represents a low-cost solution for the energy needs of many parts of the world. This will only improve as costs continue to fall, further increasing the accessibility of green energy, especially in the developing world.
How can Green Energy help the environment?
Green energy provides real environmental benefits because energy comes from natural resources such as sunlight, wind and water. Constantly replenished, these energy sources are the direct opposite of the unsustainable, carbon-emitting fossil fuels that have been powering us for over a century.
Creating energy with a zero-carbon footprint is a big step toward a greener future. If we can use it to meet our energy, industrial and transportation needs, we can greatly reduce our impact on the environment.

Benefits and Types of Green Energy
The main advantage of absolutely all alternative energy sources is their ecological compatibility. In other words, there are no harmful emissions into the atmosphere during operation of such plants. Even an accident at a wind, solar or any other alternative power plant will only lead to material losses of its owners, but will not cause a global environmental disaster, as it can happen, for example, with a nuclear power plant.
