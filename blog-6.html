Canadian city to be heated with energy from bitcoin mining
Canadian company MintGreen will heat 100 North Vancouver homes by mining cryptocurrencies. It has developed a system that can convert 96% of the energy from computer equipment into usable heat.
MintGreen has patented technology that turns the energy generated by mining bitcoins into heat for heating homes and offices. The crypto farm runs uninterrupted year-round, so this heating method is reliable and efficient.
North Vancouver boiler operator Lonsdale Energy Corporation (LEC), has decided to use clean energy sources instead of natural gas and has signed a contract with MintGreen. The innovative technology will prevent the emission of about 20 thousand tons of greenhouse gases per 1 MW of energy.
Using the heat from bitcoin mining is not a new technology. But in Canada, for the first time in the world, it will be distributed at the scale of an entire city. At first, MintGreen will heat about 100 buildings. If the testing of the system is successful, the area of application of the technology will be expanded.
Focusing on the UN's 2021 climate report, city governments in Canada have tightened regulation of carbon emissions and increased their taxation. In 2019, for example, the North Vancouver council planned to cut greenhouse gas emissions by 80 percent by 2040 and reduce them to zero by 2050.
