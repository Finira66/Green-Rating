Car Production is Now Choosing Green Energy
An automaker not only has to keep up with changing technology, consumer taste and industry standards, it must also consistently take care if the planet as it is the new challenge. The automotive industry’s move toward electrification adds another challenge — one Bentley, for example, is also taking on with its Beyond 100 initiative to become a fully electric brand by 2030 and electrified versions of all its products on offer by 2023.
In recent years, Bentley has launched hybrid SUVs and sedans on the global market that can be charged from the electric grid.
In 2021, the company introduced the Flying Spur Hybrid version, which became the most eco-friendly in the company's lineup. During the tests the car drove 733 kilometers around Iceland without any stops exclusively on renewable energy - biofuel and electricity from geothermal sources.
Electrification of the model range is part of the Beyond100 strategy that Bentley has been implementing since 2020. Its main goal is to achieve complete carbon neutrality in all of the company's operations by 2030. The strategy implies a transformation not only of the model range, but also of work operations. Including full environmental safety of the company's plant in Crewe.
Solution
Utilize solar energy and convert all plant logistics operations to fully renewable fuel.
Implementation
The company took the first steps to reduce the environmental damage from the Crewe plant even before the new strategy was developed. To reduce its environmental footprint, the company improved its recycling system and implemented a water reuse system. But the main measure was the transition to electricity exclusively from renewable sources.
Around 30,000 solar panels have been installed at the Crewe plant, 10,000 of which have been placed above the huge parking lot, the largest solar power plant in the UK. In addition, there are panels on the roofs of the production buildings.
The total capacity of the solar panels installed at the plant reaches 7.7 MW. Approximately the same amount of energy is required to serve 1,750 homes. The rest of the energy for the plant is purchased from suppliers that guarantee its environmental friendliness.
In 2020, shortly after the launch of the Beyond100 strategy, the plant moved on to the next stage. For all logistical operations, they began using exclusively renewable Green D+ fuel made from hydrogenated vegetable oil.
This is a second-generation premium biofuel. It is made from recyclable fats, vegetables, oil and other renewable resources. Compared to conventional diesel fuel, engines running on it emit 86% fewer pollutants into the environment. For example, emissions of nitrogen oxides are reduced by 30% and particulate matter by 80%.
The company has installed a 34,000-liter tank for refueling plant vehicles with biofuel. 10 trucks run on vegetable oil, transporting components between the Crewe plant and the warehouse in Winsford. The same fuel is used to fuel security vehicles and delivery vans.
Other vehicles and generators at the plant still use conventional fuel. But the company makes up for it by buying environmental credits - certificates or permits for greenhouse gas emissions.
Plans and prospects
Bentley plans to invest £2.5 billion over the next ten years to transform its product portfolio and factory in Crewe. The company envisions the site becoming a Dream Factory, a digital and flexible manufacturing facility with minimal impact on the planet. By 2030 they want to implement a go-to-zero approach that will address water, waste, and other environmental impacts.
